#BlackInTech Timeline

Here are a few inventions made by African American people.This is a timeline dating from 1800s-1900s.These inventions have all made an impact on everyday living. Every person within this timeline are famous inventors of technology. Enjoy this timeline.

1800-1899

  • (1821)- Thomas L. Jennings created the method of dry cleaning called dry scouring the first African American to receive a patent for something they created.
  • (1883)-Jan Matzeliger invented the shoe lasting machine. This invention created a surplus amount of shoes while also lowering the price of footwear
  • (1887)-Granville T Woods(aka Black Edison) invented the induction telegraph. This device sped up communications and eventually led to the prevention of serious errors such as train accidents.
  • (1892)-Sarah Boone invented the ironing board Boone's invention was made with the purpose to improve the quality in the ironing womenâ€™s garments. It was made to focus on the sleeves and body of the clothing.
  • (1896)- Charles B. Brooks invented the street sweeper. He created the truck that keeps our streets clean. Can pick up debris and other small materials.

1900-1999

  • (1920)-Walter Sammons invented the hot comb. The hot comb, created to remove the kinky curls from the hair.
  • (1922)-Garrett Morgan built the three-way stoplight, first traffic signal at a time when automobiles and horse carriages shared the road.
  • (1941)-Otis Bobby Boykin artificial cardiac pacemaker, this advice was made to control the heart's rhythm for those who had an irregular heart beat. Ironically, Otis Bobby Boykin died from heart failure later on in his life.
